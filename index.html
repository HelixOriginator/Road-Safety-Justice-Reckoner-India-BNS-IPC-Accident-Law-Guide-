About This Initiative

This is a citizen-focused, legally grounded road safety and accident response guide for India. It is designed to help people clearly understand their rights, responsibilities, and legal duties during road accidents, road rage incidents, hospital admissions, and fatalities under the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), IPC, and Motor Vehicles Act.

What To Do After a Road Accident

  • Move to safety if possible
  • Call emergency number: 112
  • Do not flee the scene
  • Click photos and videos
  • Note vehicle numbers, location and time
  • Help injured people – the law protects you
  • Cooperate with police and ambulance

Hospital Admission Rules

  • No hospital can refuse emergency treatment
  • First aid and stabilisation is mandatory
  • Paperwork cannot delay treatment
  • Identity is not required initially
  • MLC (Medico Legal Case) report must be created
  • Police must be informed by hospital

Duties of Doctors

  • Immediate emergency care is mandatory
  • No delay for police/administrative formalities
  • Prepare MLC report
  • Maintain proper records
  • Inform police in medico-legal cases
  • If patient is unconscious – implied consent applies

If Death Occurs

  • Doctor declares death and records details
  • Police conducts inquest
  • Post-mortem if required by law
  • Release of body to family after formalities
  • Issuance of death certificate
  • Family may apply for compensation (MACT / insurance)

Road Rage – Key Concerns & Suggested Reforms

  • Make severe road rage a non-bailable offence
  • Immediate licence suspension
  • High penalties and imprisonment for violent acts
  • Mandatory counselling for offenders
  • Fast-track cases using CCTV and dashcam evidence
  • Public registry for repeat offenders

Good Samaritan Law

A person who helps an injured accident victim in good faith cannot be forced into legal trouble. You are protected if you transport the victim or call emergency services.
